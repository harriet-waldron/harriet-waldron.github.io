At your request, I remember the forest

(A poem I found on the street one day.)

You are a pair of headphones in the here and now of Cuba Street. You are a winter-worn woolen jersey, hand sewn and once forgotten in a beautiful bora wardrobe. Then remembered for a week, when your friend’s mum came round for tea and cake and said you looked cold (and pale and skinny) and fuck her. You donated that jersey to the Salvation Army.
You felt good.
Really fucking good about it.
For a day. Maybe two.
Then you remembered your beautiful bluetooth headphones in the here and now of Willis Street. But you still felt IT! IT: the great non-consensual notification on your consciousness. IT was burning into your upper thigh; the abrupt vibration of your phone.
IT read: AT YOUR REQUEST
I WALKED THE NARROW FOREST
WITH A PEACH IN MY POCKET AND
A TORN OUT, SECOND-HAND COPY
OF THE LOVESONG OF J. ALFRED PRUFROCK

You remembered. There was a lamp post flickering to the bricks, as if to say, stuttering; “I’m here to give you light.” Then. A man appeared with slippered feet and silk shirt sleeves. His name was Elijah Holstein.
He held out his hand and his hand was holding an envelope.
“I found this thumb tacked to the bulletin board of a midnight cafe wall and well, I thought you should have it.”
It was a piece of paper, coffee stained and torn at the edges. It read:
If you dare, ask a stranger; what is the name of the wind?

He told you with a sad and nearly silent sigh that he was afraid. Afraid of the elemental ferocity held by the TREES, Rata and Pine; their circle of leaves, their circle of earthly dreams; their protection around the true names of nature.
You said in the there and then, something like “fuck off, I’m busy.”
but actually was, “here’s my number.” He persisted but more talking to himself.
“Perhaps I should ask the forest in synonyms and secret symbols like cherimoya apple whispered to a piece of paper then pulped and pitted into a poem then gifted and regifted on a Wednesday afternoon to the esoterically encrusted barista of Retrogrove Cafe.”
He left with a finger pointing at the moon above Mt. Victoria. You were left holding the envelope and wondering, if you should reply…
and if the barista would know how...


-Harley Bell
